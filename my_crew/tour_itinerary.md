**5-Day Tokyo Itinerary**

After considering the flight options from Paris to Tokyo on 2026-06-15 and selecting Emirates - Flight EM857 as the best value option, it's time to plan the 5-day trip to Tokyo. For accommodation, I recommend booking the **Hotel Gajoen Tokyo**, a mid-range option that offers a great balance of price, location, and amenities.

**Day 1 (2026-06-15): Arrival and Exploring Shinjuku**

* Morning: Arrive at Tokyo Narita or Haneda airport and take a train or bus to Shinjuku (approximately 1-2 hours). Check-in at the Hotel Gajoen Tokyo and freshen up.
* Afternoon: Visit the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building for a free observation deck with great views of the city (10:00 AM - 10:30 PM). Then, take a stroll through Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden, a beautiful park with gardens, walking paths, and picnic areas (9:00 AM - 4:00 PM, admission fee: 500 yen).
* Evening: Head to the Golden Gai district, a small alley filled with tiny bars and restaurants, and try some local street food and drinks. For dinner, head to **Ichiran Shibuya**, a famous ramen restaurant with a unique, rich tonkotsu (pork bone) broth (11:00 AM - 10:00 PM, price range: 890-1,000 yen).
* Estimated travel time between locations: 10-20 minutes by train or on foot.
* Advance booking notes: No tickets or reservations are required for the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building or Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden. However, it's recommended to book a table at Ichiran Shibuya in advance to avoid long wait times.

**Day 2 (2026-06-16): Asakusa, Ueno, and Akihabara**

* Morning: Take the subway to Asakusa (approximately 20 minutes) and visit Senso-ji Temple, one of the oldest and most famous temples in Japan (6:00 AM - 5:00 PM, admission fee: free, but donations are welcome). Explore the nearby Nakamise Shopping Street, a historic pedestrian street filled with traditional shops and snacks.
* Afternoon: Head to Ueno Park (approximately 10 minutes by subway) and visit the Tokyo National Museum, which features a collection of Japanese art and artifacts (9:30 AM - 5:00 PM, admission fee: 620 yen). Then, walk to the nearby Ueno Zoo, home to over 400 animals, including giant pandas (9:30 AM - 5:00 PM, admission fee: 600 yen).
* Evening: Visit Akihabara, Tokyo's electronic and anime hub, and explore the various shops and arcades. For dinner, try **Tsukemen Gyoza no Ohsho**, a restaurant specializing in tsukemen (dipping ramen) and gyoza (pan-fried dumplings) (11:30 AM - 10:30 PM, price range: 800-1,200 yen).
* Estimated travel time between locations: 10-20 minutes by subway.
* Advance booking notes: No tickets or reservations are required for Senso-ji Temple, Nakamise Shopping Street, or Ueno Park. However, it's recommended to book a table at Tsukemen Gyoza no Ohsho in advance to avoid long wait times.

**Day 3 (2026-06-17): Shibuya, Harajuku, and Omotesando**

* Morning: Take the subway to Shibuya (approximately 10 minutes) and visit the famous Shibuya Crossing. Then, take a walk around the trendy Shibuya area and visit the Shibuya 109 department store, a hub for Japanese fashion.
* Afternoon: Head to Harajuku (approximately 5 minutes by subway) and explore the colorful streets, including Takeshita-dori, a pedestrian shopping street lined with unique boutiques and snack shops. Visit the Meiji Shrine, a serene Shinto shrine located in a peaceful forested area (5:00 AM - 6:30 PM, admission fee: free, but donations are welcome).
* Evening: Walk to Omotesando, a fashionable shopping street lined with upscale boutiques and cafes. For dinner, try **Nonbei Yokocho**, a small alley filled with tiny bars and restaurants, and try some local dishes, such as yakitori (grilled chicken skewers) or okonomiyaki (savory pancake) (5:00 PM - 12:00 AM, price range: 500-1,000 yen).
* Estimated travel time between locations: 5-10 minutes by subway or on foot.
* Advance booking notes: No tickets or reservations are required for Shibuya Crossing, Shibuya 109, or Takeshita-dori. However, it's recommended to book a table at Nonbei Yokocho in advance to avoid long wait times.

**Day 4 (2026-06-18): Imperial Palace and Ginza**

* Morning: Take the subway to the Imperial Palace (approximately 10 minutes) and visit the East Garden, a beautiful traditional Japanese garden (9:00 AM - 3:00 PM, admission fee: free). Then, take a stroll around the Imperial Palace, the main residence of the Emperor of Japan.
* Afternoon: Head to Ginza (approximately 10 minutes by subway), a luxurious shopping district filled with high-end boutiques and restaurants. Visit the Ginza Mitsukoshi department store, one of the oldest and most prestigious department stores in Japan (10:00 AM - 8:00 PM, admission fee: free).
* Evening: For dinner, try **Sukiyabashi Jiro**, a three-Michelin-starred sushi restaurant run by the famous sushi chef Jiro Ono (11:30 AM - 2:00 PM, 5:00 PM - 10:00 PM, price range: 20,000-30,000 yen). Note: Advance reservations are required, and the restaurant is closed on Sundays.
* Estimated travel time between locations: 10-20 minutes by subway.
* Advance booking notes: No tickets or reservations are required for the Imperial Palace or East Garden. However, it's highly recommended to book a table at Sukiyabashi Jiro well in advance, as it is a highly acclaimed and popular restaurant.

**Day 5 (2026-06-19): Tsukiji and Odaiba**

* Morning: Take the subway to Tsukiji (approximately 20 minutes) and visit the Tsukiji Outer Market, one of the largest fish markets in the world (5:00 AM - 2:00 PM, admission fee: free). Try some fresh sushi for breakfast at one of the many restaurants in the market.
* Afternoon: Head to Odaiba (approximately 20 minutes by subway), a man-made island in Tokyo Bay, and visit the Miraikan science museum, which features interactive exhibits on science and technology (10:00 AM - 5:00 PM, admission fee: 2,000 yen). Then, take a walk along the beach and enjoy the views of Tokyo Bay.
* Evening: For dinner, try **Tsukiji Itadoraku**, a restaurant specializing in fresh seafood and sushi (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM, price range: 1,000-2,000 yen).
* Estimated travel time between locations: 20 minutes by subway.
* Advance booking notes: No tickets or reservations are required for the Tsukiji Outer Market or Odaiba. However, it's recommended to book a table at Tsukiji Itadoraku in advance to avoid long wait times.
