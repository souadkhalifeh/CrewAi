**Comprehensive Travel Advice for a Trip from Paris to Tokyo**

As you prepare for your trip from Paris to Tokyo on 2026-06-15, it's essential to consider several factors, including visa and entry requirements, weather, local currency, safety, cultural etiquette, and essential items to pack.

**Visa and Entry Requirements:**

Before your trip, ensure you have a valid passport with at least six months' validity. Citizens of France do not require a visa to enter Japan for tourism or business stays of up to 90 days. However, you will need to provide proof of onward travel, sufficient funds, and a return ticket. It's also recommended to register with the French embassy in Tokyo and obtain travel insurance that covers Japan.

**Best Time to Visit and Weather:**

Tokyo's climate is temperate, with four distinct seasons. June is the start of summer in Tokyo, with average high temperatures ranging from 22°C to 25°C (72°F to 77°F). Humidity levels can be high, and there may be occasional rain showers. Pack accordingly, with lightweight clothing, comfortable shoes, and a waterproof jacket or umbrella.

**Local Currency and Tipping Customs:**

The local currency in Japan is the Japanese yen (JPY). You can exchange your money at airports, banks, or currency exchange machines. Credit cards, such as Visa and Mastercard, are widely accepted in Tokyo, but it's still a good idea to have some cash on hand. Tipping is not customary in Japan, and in some cases, it may even be considered impolite.

**Safety Tips and Areas to be Aware of:**

Tokyo is generally a safe city, with low crime rates compared to other major cities. However, it's still essential to take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and tourist hotspots. Avoid traveling alone in unfamiliar areas at night, especially in the Shinjuku and Shibuya districts. Additionally, be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded train stations and markets.

**Cultural Etiquette and Local Customs:**

Japan is known for its unique culture and customs. Here are a few essential tips to keep in mind:

* Bowing: Bowing is a traditional Japanese greeting, with the depth and duration of the bow depending on the situation. A slight bow of the head is a common greeting, while a deeper, longer bow is used for more formal situations.
* Table manners: Wait for the host to start eating before you begin, and use chopsticks correctly (don't leave them standing upright in your rice bowl).
* Gift-giving: If you're invited to a Japanese home, bring a gift, such as fruit or chocolates. Wrap the gift nicely and receive it with both hands.
* Dress modestly: Avoid revealing clothing, especially when visiting temples or shrines.

**Must-Know Phrases in the Local Language:**

While many Tokyo residents speak some English, it's still helpful to learn basic Japanese phrases:

* Hello: (konnichiwa)
* Thank you: (arigatou)
* Excuse me: (sumimasen)
* Sorry: (gomen nasai)
* Yes: (hai)
* No: (iie)
